### Event Definitions

- **shot**: A puck propelled toward the **{goalie_color}** goalie's net with
  the visible intent to score, that travels along a path that would have entered
  the net if not for the goalie, the posts, the crossbar, or a defender's body.
  This is the BROAD category and includes:
    ✓ Pucks the goalie stops (these are also `shotsOnNet`).
    ✓ Pucks that go in (also `shotsOnNet` and `goals`).
    ✓ Pucks that miss the net wide or high (NOT `shotsOnNet`).
    ✓ Pucks blocked by a {goalie_color}-team skater BEFORE reaching the goalie,
      where the trajectory clearly was toward the net.
    ✓ Hard rebounds shot back at the net (each shot attempt counts once).

  **DO NOT count any of the following as a shot:**
    ✗ Dump-ins from the neutral zone or just inside the blue line — pucks
      flipped or rimmed into the offensive zone for a line change or to be
      retrieved. These are NOT shots even if they travel toward the net area.
    ✗ Centring passes / cross-ice passes that happen to travel near the net.
      A puck moving across the slot to set up a teammate is a pass, not a shot.
    ✗ Wraparound attempts that never get a clean shot off (puck dies behind
      the net or is pushed into the corner).
    ✗ Puck movement into the zone where no skater commits to a shooting motion.
    ✗ A skater carrying the puck behind the net or into the corner.
    ✗ Puck deflections off skates, sticks, or pads in front of the net that
      were not preceded by a deliberate shot motion.
    ✗ Any puck movement during a stoppage (after a whistle, before a faceoff).

  **Test for "was that a shot?":** Ask "did a {opponent_color} player
  visibly wind up, snap, or release the puck with the body mechanics of a
  shot, AND was the puck's path consistent with trying to score?"
  If you cannot answer YES to BOTH questions, it is not a shot.

  **Rebound and scramble chains — count carefully:** When the goalie
  makes a save and the puck stays loose in front of the net (a scramble
  or rebound sequence), do NOT count every poke, every kick-out, or
  every pad-block as a new shot. The rule:
    ✓ Count the originating shot (1 shot, 1 SOG, 1 save).
    ✓ Count a rebound as a SECOND shot only if a {opponent_color} player
      gains clear possession of the loose puck and executes a NEW
      shooting motion (wind-up, snap, or release) at it from a
      distinguishably different position OR after the puck has come to
      rest. Tic-tac-toe re-attacks count.
    ✗ Do NOT count goalie pad-saves, blocker deflections, body smothers,
      or stick-pokes during the scramble as separate shots — these are
      the goalie continuing to handle the same originating shot's
      aftermath.
    ✗ Do NOT count incidental puck contacts in a goalmouth scrum as
      shots. If the puck is bouncing off bodies in the crease without a
      shooter committing to a clean release, no new shot has occurred.
    ✗ Do NOT count a goalie's freeze of a loose puck as a save of a new
      shot — it ends the original shot.

  **Calibration anchor:** A typical 120-second clip of legitimate
  offensive zone pressure contains 1–4 actual shots. Most clips contain
  0–2. **A clip with 6+ shots is rare and requires the level of action
  you would see in a power play with sustained shooting.** If you find
  yourself counting more than 4 shots in a 120-second clip, re-audit
  every one against the exclusions above — most of them are probably
  passes, dump-ins, or zone-time motion, not shots.

  **Anti-narration check for `shots` (CRITICAL — v13 evidence-anchored
  truth table — do this LAST before returning the count):**

  v13 replaces v11's "enumerate every shot attempt" instruction with an
  evidence-based truth table. Background: a 14-video probe (Jan 2026)
  found that under v11 framing Gemini produces 2.76 shots per real-shot
  clip — over-counting by ~2× even on clean videos. The probe ALSO
  found four features that cleanly discriminate real shots from
  zone-pressure noise when asked atomically:

    Discriminator                       Real-shot rate    Noise rate    Disc
    ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
    puck_traveling_toward_net           0.82              0.00          +0.82
    puck_release_frame_visible          0.71              0.10          +0.61
    puck_impact_sound_audible           0.71              0.20          +0.51
    shot_lane_clear_to_net              0.47              0.00          +0.47
    ────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
    puck_carrier_holds_or_passes        0.12              0.70          −0.58
    (anti-anchor — fires on noise)

  The fix v13 makes: instead of asking "list every shot you see," ask
  Gemini to evaluate candidate shot moments against the truth table
  below. The required-feature rule kills the over-counting that v11
  could not constrain by prompt wording alone.

  **TRUTH TABLE FOR SHOTS:**

  A candidate is a real shot if it satisfies ALL of:

    REQUIRED (must be TRUE):
      `feature_puck_traveling_toward_net`
        — At or immediately after the candidate moment, the puck
          visibly travels in a straight or curved line TOWARD the
          goal net (vs. along the ice between players for a pass,
          or staying on the carrier's stick for a deke/carry).

    EVIDENCE (at least ONE must be TRUE):
      `feature_puck_release_frame_visible`
        — There is a clear puck-LEAVES-STICK frame: the player's
          stick visibly contacts and releases the puck. Distinguish
          from a pass (puck travels along the ice between teammates)
          or carry (puck stays on the stick).
      `feature_puck_impact_sound_audible`
        — A crisp puck impact sound: puck-on-pad, puck-on-post,
          puck-on-glass. Distinguish from skate scraping, body
          contact, or stick-on-stick.

    DISQUALIFIER (must be FALSE):
      `feature_puck_carrier_holds_or_passes`
        — The dominant action at the candidate moment is the
          puck-carrier HOLDING the puck (maintaining possession,
          looking for an opening) or PASSING to a teammate (puck
          travels along the ice). If this is true, the candidate
          is zone pressure or possession, not a shot — even if
          the puck eventually goes elsewhere later.

  **HOW TO USE THE TRUTH TABLE (process):**

  1. Watch the clip and identify every CANDIDATE moment where a puck
     attempt toward the net might have happened. Mark a candidate
     timestamp (MM:SS within the clip) for each.

  2. For EACH candidate, evaluate the four boolean features above.
     Commit to all four booleans before deciding whether the candidate
     is a real shot.

  3. The candidate becomes a real shot ONLY IF:
       REQUIRED is TRUE
       AND ≥1 EVIDENCE feature is TRUE
       AND DISQUALIFIER is FALSE

  4. List the surviving real shots in `shot_timestamps`. Set the
     `shots` count from `len(shot_timestamps)`. Each entry must include
     the four boolean feature values that justified it.

  **What this protects against:**
    - "Sustained zone pressure" doesn't fire the truth table because
      `feature_puck_carrier_holds_or_passes` is TRUE during possession.
    - "Scramble in front of net" doesn't fire unless a specific
      candidate moment shows the puck actually traveling toward the
      net with a visible release or impact sound.
    - "Two close shots that are really one shot + rebound" — the second
      candidate still requires its own visible release frame OR impact
      sound, so a phantom rebound that doesn't produce one is rejected.

  **Calibration anchor:** In a typical 45-second threat window, 1-3
  candidates SURVIVING the truth table is normal. 4+ surviving in
  45 seconds is rare even on a power play — if you see this, re-check
  whether each candidate independently satisfies the REQUIRED feature
  (it must travel toward the net, not toward a teammate).

  **If `shots == 0`:** return `shot_timestamps: []`. Do not invent
  candidates to populate the field. A clip with sustained possession
  but no clear release frame and no impact sound HAS NO SHOTS in it.

  **It is much better to UNDER-count than to inflate.** When in doubt
  on REQUIRED or DISQUALIFIER, return false and drop the candidate.
  The truth table is INTENTIONALLY strict.

  **Structured shot enumeration (extended for v13 — required for
  every entry in shot_timestamps):**

  For each shot that survives the truth table:
    1. `timestamp`: MM:SS within this clip — the moment the puck is
       released (the snap / wrist-flick / slap contact frame), NOT the
       moment the goalie reacts or the puck reaches the net.
    2. `location`: from the taxonomy — "right point," "high slot,"
       "left circle," "low slot," "right circle," "left point,"
       "right wing," "left wing," "in close," "behind net," or
       similar.
    3. `release`: "slap shot," "wrist shot," "snap shot," "one-timer,"
       "redirect/tip," "backhand."
    4. `outcome`: one of:
       - "goal" — puck crossed the goal line (consistent with goals>=1
         from the goal-detection logic)
       - "save" — goalie stopped the shot
       - "miss" — shot went wide of the net or over the crossbar
         (counted in `shots` but NOT in `shotsOnNet`)
       - "blocked" — defender intercepted before the shot reached the
         goalie (counted in `shots` but NOT in `shotsOnNet`)
    5. `feature_puck_traveling_toward_net`: true (must be true — this
       is the REQUIRED feature; if false the candidate would not be
       a shot)
    6. `feature_puck_release_frame_visible`: true | false (EVIDENCE)
    7. `feature_puck_impact_sound_audible`: true | false (EVIDENCE)
    8. `feature_puck_carrier_holds_or_passes`: false (must be false —
       this is the DISQUALIFIER; if true the candidate would have
       been rejected)

  At least one of `feature_puck_release_frame_visible` and
  `feature_puck_impact_sound_audible` MUST be true for every entry.

- **shotOnNet (SOG)**: A shot (as defined above) where the puck reached
  the goalie's body, equipment, the net itself, or crossed the goal line.
  This is the NARROWER category and is counted by what the GOALIE had to
  deal with.

  **A shot is `shotOnNet` only if you can identify a specific moment
  where ONE of the following happens:**
    ✓ The goalie makes a save (glove catch, blocker deflection, pad save,
      stick save, body block, freezing the puck).
    ✓ The puck enters the net (this is also a goal).
    ✓ The puck strikes the post or crossbar AND would have entered the
      net otherwise. (Post hits that go directly out of the zone don't
      count; post hits where the puck stays in the crease and is then
      played do count.)

  **`shotOnNet` is NOT:**
    ✗ A shot that misses the net wide or high — even if just barely.
    ✗ A shot blocked by a {goalie_color}-team skater before reaching the
      goalie. The skater took it; the goalie didn't have to make a play.
    ✗ A pass / centring puck that the goalie tracks but doesn't actively
      stop.

  **Calibration anchor:** `shotsOnNet` is typically 40–70% of `shots` in
  a real game. If you have `shots = 10` and `shotsOnNet = 9`, you are
  almost certainly over-counting `shotsOnNet`. Re-check each: did the
  goalie actually have to make a save?

  **Anti-narration check (CRITICAL — do this LAST before returning counts):**
  After producing your `shotsOnNet` count, force yourself to enumerate
  each one. If your decision_notes contain phrases like "multiple shots
  on net, all saved" or "sustained pressure with several shots" or "a
  series of saves," that is a NARRATIVE, not a count. A real count means
  you can answer: "save 1 happened at timestamp X with the goalie's
  [glove/blocker/pad/stick]; save 2 happened at timestamp Y with the
  goalie's [...]," and so on, for every save you claim.

  **The rule:** If you cannot list a distinct timestamp and
  body-part-of-save for each of N claimed `shotsOnNet`, your N is too
  high. Drop the count to the number you CAN enumerate concretely. It
  is far better to under-count than to inflate from narrative.

  **Symptom check:** if `shotsOnNet` equals `shots` (100% conversion
  rate), this is almost always wrong. In real hockey, a meaningful
  fraction of shots miss wide, miss high, or are blocked by a defender.
  Re-audit your `shots`: are some of them actually misses-wide that
  should NOT be counted as `shotsOnNet`?

- **shotOnNet vs save:** Every save is a shotOnNet. Not every shotOnNet
  is a save — goals and posts-that-would-have-gone-in are also shotOnNet.

- **save**: The goalie stopping a shot on net. A goal is NOT a save.

- **rebound**: A loose, playable puck coming off the goalie after a save.
  A puck frozen immediately by the goalie is NOT a rebound.

- **goal**: The puck fully crosses the goal line against the **{goalie_color}**
  goalie. Goals are rare — a typical game has 2–6 total goals across both ends.
  Most clips contain zero goals. **Default to 0.**

  **Do NOT count as a goal:**
  - A shot taken directly off a faceoff that the goalie saves.
  - A team winning a faceoff cleanly and maintaining possession.

- **Scoreboard:** A scoreboard is NOT evidence of a goal unless you see the
  score CHANGE within this clip. A static score reflects a prior event.

### Goal Detection Signals

The features below come in two groups: **v10 path features** drive the
goal-detection truth table; **legacy features** are kept in the JSON
output for backward compatibility but are NOT used by v10 paths.

#### v10 path features (drive the truth table)

  🆕 **scoreboard_change**: A visible scoreboard digit (numeric goal
     count) changes during the clip. Set true ONLY if you can identify:
     1. A specific scoreboard or score-overlay visible in the clip
     2. A digit in the goal count (not period, not time, not shots) that
        changes from one number to another (e.g. "1 → 2" or "0 → 1")
     If the scoreboard is partially obscured, off-screen, or the digit
     change is ambiguous, set false. This is the strongest goal anchor
     in v10 because scoreboard changes are objective state changes —
     hard to fabricate.

  🆕 **attacking_team_skates_to_bench**: After a stoppage, multiple
     players from the SCORING team visibly skate together toward their
     own bench in a fist-bump line — the standard post-goal celebration
     ritual. Set true ONLY if you see:
     1. 2+ players skating toward the bench (not just one player going
        for a line change)
     2. The pattern resembles a celebration line (close together,
        slow-coast pace, glove bumps with bench players visible or
        implied), not a routine line change
     This signal is highly specific to goals and rarely fires on saves
     or near-misses.

  🆕 **crowd_cheer_sustained**: A sustained crowd cheer audible for
     more than 3 seconds, rising clearly above baseline noise. Set true
     ONLY if:
     1. The cheer is clearly distinct from baseline crowd murmur
     2. The cheer continues for >3 seconds (not a brief gasp or surprise
        reaction)
     3. The cheer rises to celebration intensity, not just stays at
        elevated near-miss intensity
     A brief 1-second crowd reaction does NOT set this to true.

  🔑 **anchor_ref_points_at_net (D)**: A referee extends ONE arm to point
     directly at the **{goalie_color}** net. Distinguish carefully:
     - **Icing:** arm points down-ice toward the far end
     - **Waved off:** BOTH arms sweep across the body
     - **Penalty:** ONE arm raised UPWARD above the head
     Only set true if unambiguously pointing AT this goalie's net.

#### Legacy features (in schema for compatibility; NOT used by v10 paths)

  ❌ **anchor_puck_crosses_line — REMOVED in v8.** Always set to false.
     Always set `anchor_puck_crosses_line_timestamp` to empty string.

  ❌ **anchor_puck_retrieved_from_net (N) — DEMOTED in v10.** Earlier
     versions used this as a strong anchor, but probe testing showed
     Gemini fires it on 75% of FP clips when in goal-detection context
     (vs 25% in atomic-observation context). Still observed and reported
     for diagnostic purposes, but does NOT drive goal confirmation in
     v10. Apply the same strict definition as before:
     - Set true ONLY when a player or referee visibly reaches INTO the
       net through the front opening to remove the puck.
     - Set false for goalie pad-handling, defender clearing, etc.

  ❌ **support_centre_ice_faceoff (FO) — DEMOTED in v10.** Same probe
     finding: 33% atomic FP rate inflates to 75% in goal-detection
     framing. Still observed and reported, does NOT drive goal
     confirmation in v10. Continue to apply the strict definition
     (both teams visibly at centre ice for the next faceoff).

  ❌ **support_celebration (B) — DEMOTED in v10.** Fires on 95%+ of
     FP clips in the probe. Still observed and reported but no longer
     a path requirement. Apply the same definition (2+ scoring-team
     players visibly celebrating).

  ❌ **support_whistle (C) — DEMOTED in v10.** Whistles fire constantly
     in hockey clips (saves, icings, penalties, period ends). Still
     observed and reported, not used by v10 paths.

  ❌ **support_crowd_spike — REPLACED by `crowd_cheer_sustained`** in
     v10. Brief crowd spikes are too easy to claim. The v10 feature
     requires sustained (>3s) elevated cheering. Still observed for
     compatibility, but `crowd_cheer_sustained` is the v10 signal.

  ❌ **disqualifier_active**: still used. Set true if any of the
     disqualifying conditions in Step 3 fire (penalty box, raised
     arm signal, play continues without stoppage, save observed
     immediately before celebration).

### Goal Detection Process

Goals are rare; default to `goals: 0`. A typical game has 2–6 total goals
across both ends, so most clips contain zero goals. Be conservative.

All confirmed goals require TWO things:
  (a) A valid evidence combination from the truth table below.
  (b) A concrete `confirming_detail` you can describe — a specific fact
      about THIS clip that ties your goal claim to something real (which
      team celebrated, what jersey colours, where the puck was retrieved,
      which ref pointed, etc.). Generic language like "the team celebrated
      and the ref pointed" is NOT a confirming_detail.

Follow these steps in strict order.

---

**STEP 1 — UNCONDITIONAL OVERRIDE:**

If a referee waves **BOTH arms simultaneously across the body** (waved off)
at any point → goals = 0, disqualifier_active = true. Stop here.

---

**STEP 2 — GOAL AT CLIP START:**

If support_celebration is visible within the first 5 seconds, a goal may have
just preceded the clip. Because the scoring event itself is outside the clip,
you cannot see A (puck crossing the line). Confirmation requires the
AFTER-GOAL routine to be visible in this clip:

  **BOTH** of the following must be true:
    1. `anchor_puck_retrieved_from_net` (N) = true — a player or ref visibly
       pulls the puck from INSIDE the net.
    2. `support_centre_ice_faceoff` = true — both teams visibly skate to and
       line up at the centre-ice red line for the faceoff.

  A ref point (D) alone is NOT sufficient — refs point at nets on saves,
  penalties, and icings. A whistle (C) alone is NOT sufficient.

  If N AND faceoff are both true → goals = 1. Stop here.
  Otherwise → continue to Step 3.

---

**STEP 3 — MAIN GOAL DETECTION LOGIC:**

A clip confirms a goal ONLY through one of the two paths in the v10
truth table below. v10 uses an evidence-based design: paths are anchored
on features that probe testing showed Gemini observes consistently
across atomic and goal-detection contexts. Earlier paths (Path 4 etc.)
were anchored on `puck_retrieved_from_net` and `centre_ice_faceoff_visible`
which probe testing showed Gemini will inflate from ~25-33% atomic FP
rate to ~75% under goal-detection framing — making them unreliable as
path anchors. v10 uses different anchors.

**Note on `A` (puck crosses line):** removed in v8 and remains removed.
The schema fields `anchor_puck_crosses_line` and
`anchor_puck_crosses_line_timestamp` should ALWAYS be set to false /
empty string respectively.

**Truth table — these are the ONLY ways a goal is confirmed:**

| Path | scoreboard_change | ref_points_at_net | attacking_team_skates_to_bench | crowd_cheer_sustained | Name |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| B | T | T | any | any | scoreboard + ref point |
| C | any | any | T | T | bench skate + sustained cheer |

A goal is confirmed if EITHER Path B OR Path C is satisfied AND no
disqualifier fires.

Every other signal combination → goals = 0.

Notable removed paths (do NOT confirm goals under any circumstance):
  - **All paths anchored on A** (puck-crosses-line / red-light) — removed
    in v8.
  - **Path 6 (B + D + N alone)** — removed in v8.
  - **Path 3 (B + D + FO)** — removed in v9.
  - **Path 7 (B + C + D + crowd_spike)** — removed in v9.
  - **Path 4 (B + D + N + FO, the v9 production path)** — removed in v10
    because probe testing showed both N and FO are gameable when used as
    path anchors. The features still appear in the schema for diagnostic
    purposes but do not drive goal confirmation.
  - **Any path without scoreboard_change OR (bench + crowd).** v10
    requires one of these two empirically-discriminating combinations.

**Hallucination guards for v10 paths:**

  🛑 **scoreboard_change is strict.** Set true ONLY if you can identify
     a specific scoreboard or score-overlay digit (the goal count, NOT
     period or time or shots) visibly changing during the clip. Apply
     these checks:
     - You must be able to point to where on the screen the scoreboard
       is, BEFORE the change and AFTER the change.
     - The change must be in the GOAL COUNT digit specifically — not
       period clock, not shot count, not penalty count.
     - If the scoreboard is too small, partially obscured, off-screen,
       or you can't read the specific digit, set false.

  🛑 **attacking_team_skates_to_bench is strict.** Set true ONLY if:
     - 2+ players from the SAME team skate together toward their bench.
     - The pattern is clearly a celebration line (slow coast, gloves
       visible, fist-bump motion implied or visible), NOT a routine
       line change.
     - This must occur AFTER a stoppage in play, not during continuous
       action.
     A routine shift change (one player goes off, one comes on) does
     NOT satisfy this signal.

  🛑 **crowd_cheer_sustained is strict.** Set true ONLY if:
     - The cheer rises clearly above baseline crowd noise.
     - The cheer is sustained for >3 seconds.
     - The cheer is at celebration intensity (sustained roar), not
       at near-miss intensity (brief gasp + return to baseline).
     A brief 1-second crowd reaction or "ooh" does NOT set this true.

  🛑 **Negative anchor — no save observed.** If you observe the
     {goalie_color} goalie clearly making a save in the seconds
     immediately before the apparent goal moment — gloving the puck,
     freezing it under the body, kicking it out, smothering it — set
     `disqualifier_active = true` and goals = 0. The probe showed this
     check is unreliable when used as a Path 7 guard, but as a Path B/C
     disqualifier it provides belt-and-suspenders safety.

  🛑 **`confirming_detail` MUST describe the specific path moment.**
     If you claim Path B, your confirming_detail must describe (a) where
     on-screen the scoreboard is and what the digit changed from/to, AND
     (b) the ref point moment. If you claim Path C, your confirming_detail
     must describe (a) which players skated to which bench at what
     timestamp, AND (b) when the crowd cheer started and how long it
     lasted.

  🛑 Examples of acceptable confirming_detail:
       ✓ "Path B: scoreboard in upper-left changed from '0' to '1' on
          the home-team side at 00:31; ref's right arm extended toward
          the {goalie_color} net at 00:30."
       ✓ "Path C: 4 players in white skated together toward the
          home-team bench from 00:33-00:38 in a fist-bump line;
          sustained crowd cheer audible from 00:31-00:42."
     Examples of UNACCEPTABLE generic detail (reject these — set goals=0):
       ✗ "Scoreboard changed and the ref pointed"
       ✗ "Players celebrated and crowd cheered"
       ✗ "A goal was scored"
     If your confirming_detail reads like generic commentary, the claim
     is not valid. Set goals = 0.

**Disqualifiers** (force goals = 0 even if Path B or Path C signals fire):
  - Player visibly skates to the penalty box after the stoppage
  - Referee raises ONE arm UPWARD (penalty signal, not a goal signal)
  - Play visibly continues on the ice without stoppage after the claimed
    goal moment for more than 5 seconds (save or play-on, not a goal)
  - **{goalie_color} goalie observed making a save** in the seconds
    immediately before the apparent goal moment (gloving, freezing,
    smothering, pad-clearing the puck out)
  If any disqualifier is observed → goals = 0, disqualifier_active = true.

---

**🛑 ANTI-RATIONALIZATION RULE — DO NOT BACK INTO A GOAL VIA THE SOG IDENTITY:**

The consistency rule `shotsOnNet = saves + goals` is for COUNT BOOKKEEPING
ONLY. It is NEVER a basis for inferring that a goal occurred.

**Goals are confirmed only by an explicit truth-table path (Path B or
Path C) above.** If you observed a shot-on-net but cannot satisfy any
truth-table path, that shot is a SAVE. The goalie is presumed to have made
the save unless the truth table positively confirms otherwise.

If you find yourself thinking ANY of the following, STOP and set goals = 0:
  ✗ "A goal must be counted to maintain the shotsOnNet=saves+goals identity"
  ✗ "The shot crossed the line and the goalie didn't visibly save it, so it
    must be a goal"
  ✗ "If ruled no-goal the counts become inconsistent"
  ✗ "By elimination this must be a goal"
  ✗ "The puck entered the net even though no truth-table path is satisfied"

**Resolution rule when a SOG cannot satisfy a truth-table path:**
  - Set goals = 0
  - Set saves = shotsOnNet (the shot was a save the goalie handled)
  - The identity holds with goals=0: shotsOnNet = saves + 0 = saves
  - Do NOT increase goals to balance the equation
  - Do NOT decrease shotsOnNet to balance the equation either

A clean save without a confirmed goal is the DEFAULT outcome of a shot-on-net.
Confirmed goals are rare; defaulting to "goalie saved it" is correct on
unclear footage. Inflating goals to satisfy a count identity is a hallucination,
not analysis.

**STEP 4 — ZONE vs CENTRE-ICE FACEOFF:**

┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  ZONE FACEOFF = NO GOAL                                                  │
│  Players line up on a dot INSIDE one zone, within the blue line.         │
│  The defending goalie is visible nearby at their net.                    │
│  Both teams clustered at ONE end. Only one blue line is visible.         │
│                                                                          │
│  CENTRE-ICE FACEOFF = GOAL CONFIRMED                                     │
│  Players from BOTH teams skate toward the MIDDLE of the ice.             │
│  Line up on or near the large RED CENTRE CIRCLE at the midpoint.         │
│  Both goalies visible at OPPOSITE ends. Ice looks balanced.              │
│  The wide red centre line is visible across the full width.              │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

### Few-Shot Examples

**Example 1: Strong Evidence Goal — Path B (scoreboard + ref point)**
Scenario: A shot is taken on the {goalie_color} net. The puck appears to
enter the net (camera angle ambiguous). The scoreboard in the upper-right
of the frame visibly changes from "0" to "1" on the home-team side at
00:32. A ref's right arm extends toward the {goalie_color} net at 00:31.
Players celebrate.
Signals: scoreboard_change=true, anchor_ref_points_at_net=true,
attacking_team_skates_to_bench=true, crowd_cheer_sustained=false
Path satisfied: Path B (scoreboard_change AND ref_points_at_net)
confirming_detail: "Scoreboard in upper-right corner changed from '0' to
'1' on the home-team digit at 00:32; ref's right arm extended toward the
{goalie_color} net at 00:31."
Result: goals=1

**Example 2: Strong Evidence Goal — Path C (bench + crowd)**
Scenario: A shot is taken on the {goalie_color} net. After play stops,
4 players in white skate together toward the home-team bench from
00:33-00:38 in a fist-bump line. Sustained crowd cheer audible from
00:31-00:42. Scoreboard not visible in the clip frame.
Signals: scoreboard_change=false, anchor_ref_points_at_net=false,
attacking_team_skates_to_bench=true, crowd_cheer_sustained=true
Path satisfied: Path C (attacking_team_skates_to_bench AND
crowd_cheer_sustained)
confirming_detail: "4 players in white skated together toward the home-team
bench from 00:33-00:38 in a clear fist-bump line; sustained crowd cheer
audible from 00:31-00:42 rising clearly above baseline."
Result: goals=1

**Example 3: Both Paths Satisfied (strongest)**
Scenario: Goal scored. Scoreboard changes 1→2. Ref points at the
{goalie_color} net. Bench fist-bump line. Sustained crowd cheer.
Signals: scoreboard_change=true, anchor_ref_points_at_net=true,
attacking_team_skates_to_bench=true, crowd_cheer_sustained=true
Path satisfied: Both Path B and Path C
Result: goals=1
confirming_detail: should describe both the scoreboard moment AND the
bench-skate / crowd moment.

**Example 4: Rejected — celebration + ref point but no scoreboard or bench**
Scenario: Players celebrate. Ref points at the net. But neither the
scoreboard changes (or scoreboard is not visible), nor do players
skate to the bench in a fist-bump line, nor is there a sustained crowd
cheer (only a brief reaction).
Signals: scoreboard_change=false, anchor_ref_points_at_net=true,
attacking_team_skates_to_bench=false, crowd_cheer_sustained=false
Result: goals=0, decision_notes="Celebration and ref point present but
neither Path B (no scoreboard change) nor Path C (no bench fist-bump
and no sustained cheer) is satisfied. v10 requires one of these
empirically-discriminating signal combinations."

**Example 5: Rejected — save followed by rebound (the v8 FP pattern)**
Scenario: Shot on the {goalie_color} net. Goalie makes a clear glove
save. Defender clears. Players raise sticks toward the crease in
frustration; ref points to indicate covered-puck stoppage. Brief
moderate crowd reaction. No scoreboard change visible. No bench
fist-bump line — players regroup individually.
Signals: scoreboard_change=false, anchor_ref_points_at_net=true,
attacking_team_skates_to_bench=false, crowd_cheer_sustained=false
disqualifier_active=true (goalie save observed)
Result: goals=0, decision_notes="Goalie made a glove save before the
apparent celebration. Disqualifier fires regardless of path signals.
This was the dominant FP pattern in v7/v8."

**Example 6: Rejected — generic confirming_detail**
Scenario: Model claims a goal via Path B but writes confirming_detail
as "Scoreboard changed and ref pointed at the net."
Correct handling: Even if the path signals fire, generic descriptions
that could apply to any goal don't establish the model actually
observed the moment. Set goals=0.
Result: goals=0

**Example 7: Rejected — Path 4 signals fire but no v10 path**
Scenario: Player appears to retrieve the puck from inside the net.
Both teams skate to centre ice. Players celebrate. Ref points at the
net. Whistle. But the scoreboard does NOT change (or is not visible),
no bench fist-bump line, and crowd reaction is brief (not sustained).
Signals: anchor_puck_retrieved_from_net=true, support_centre_ice_faceoff=true,
support_celebration=true, anchor_ref_points_at_net=true,
scoreboard_change=false, attacking_team_skates_to_bench=false,
crowd_cheer_sustained=false
Correct handling: The legacy v9 Path 4 (B+D+N+FO) was removed in v10
because probe testing showed N and FO are gameable. Even with all the
v9 Path 4 signals firing, v10 requires Path B or Path C. Set goals=0.
Result: goals=0, decision_notes="Legacy Path 4 signals present but no
v10 path satisfied. Probe testing showed N and FO inflate to ~75% on
FP clips when used as path anchors. v10 requires scoreboard_change OR
the bench+crowd combination."

**Example 8: Shot counting — busy 120-second sustained zone time**
Scenario: A 120-second clip of {opponent_color} sustained pressure. You see:
  - {opponent_color} player carries the puck in over the blue line and
    dumps it into the corner (NOT a shot — dump-in)
  - {opponent_color} player retrieves the puck and passes across the slot
    to the high circle (NOT a shot — pass)
  - {opponent_color} player at the right point winds up and rips a
    slap shot; goalie makes a glove save (1 shot, 1 SOG, 1 save)
  - The save creates a rebound; {opponent_color} player at the left dot
    takes a quick wrist shot at the open side; goalie kicks it out with
    a pad (2 shots, 2 SOG, 2 saves)
  - Loose puck deflects off a {goalie_color} skater's shin pad without
    any shooting motion preceding it (NOT a shot — incidental deflection)
  - {opponent_color} player from the high slot takes a shot that misses
    wide-right of the net (3 shots, still 2 SOG)
  - Puck rims around the boards and is cleared by {goalie_color} (NOT a
    shot — rim is not a shot)
Correct counts: shots=3, shotsOnNet=2, saves=2, rebounds=1, goals=0.
Wrong counts (over-counting): shots=8, shotsOnNet=6, saves=6.
The wrong counts include the dump-in, the pass, the deflection, and the
rim — none of which pass the "wind up, snap, or release" test for a shot.

**Example 8: Shot counting — quiet 120-second clip**
Scenario: A 120-second clip where the camera shows mostly neutral-zone
play with one breakaway by {opponent_color}. The breakaway player skates
in alone, snaps a wrist shot from the high slot — goalie makes a kick save.
No other shooting motions in the clip.
Correct counts: shots=1, shotsOnNet=1, saves=1, rebounds=0, goals=0.
Wrong counts (over-counting): shots=4, shotsOnNet=3.
The "wrong" counts often arise from labelling pre-shot puck movement and
the rebound clear-out as additional "shots" — they aren't.

**Example 9: Shot counting — rebound and scramble chain**
Scenario: A 90-second clip where {opponent_color} cycles the puck in the
offensive zone. You see:
  - Player at the right boards passes to the slot — a {opponent_color}
    forward redirects/snaps it on net (1 shot).
  - Goalie makes a glove save but doesn't freeze it — puck pops loose
    in front of crease (1 SOG, 1 save, rebound=1).
  - {opponent_color} player crashes the net and pokes at the loose puck
    while the goalie smothers — goalie pad-blocks the poke. NOT a new
    shot — this is goalie handling the same originating shot.
  - Goalie freezes the puck. Whistle. (Originating shot is now closed.)
  - Faceoff in the offensive zone — {opponent_color} wins it cleanly,
    defenseman one-times a slap shot from the point. Goalie blocker save.
    (2nd shot, 2nd SOG, 2nd save).
  - Rebound caroms to the corner; {goalie_color} player retrieves and
    clears. (NOT a new shot — no new shooting motion.)
Correct counts: shots=2, shotsOnNet=2, saves=2, rebounds=1, goals=0.
Wrong counts (over-counting): shots=5, shotsOnNet=5, saves=5.
The wrong count happens when the model treats every poke and pad-save
during the scramble as a separate shot/save event. The rule: a save
scramble is one originating shot until the goalie freezes the puck or
a NEW shooter executes a fresh wind-up/snap/release.

**Example 10: Shot counting — long busy zone time, over-counting trap**
Scenario: A 120-second clip of {opponent_color} sustained pressure.
The model is tempted to write decision_notes like "several shots, many
saves" and report shots=10, shotsOnNet=5, saves=5. Forcing per-shot
enumeration reveals what was actually visible:
  - 00:14, slap shot from right point — goalie glove save (1 shot, 1 SOG, 1 save)
  - 00:18, rebound poke at the loose puck by a {opponent_color} forward
    while the goalie smothers — NOT a new shot (scramble noise)
  - 00:22, goalie freezes the puck. Whistle. (originating shot closes)
  - 00:35–00:55, zone time: cycles, two cross-ice passes, a dump-in
    around the boards retrieved by the defending team — NO shots
    (passes and zone movement are not shots)
  - 01:08, wrist shot from the high slot — goalie pad save (2 shots, 2 SOG, 2 saves)
  - 01:25–01:40, more cycling, no clean release — NO shots
  - 01:52, slap shot from the left point misses wide of the net
    (3 shots, still 2 SOG)
Correct counts: shots=3, shotsOnNet=2, saves=2, rebounds=1, goals=0.
The model's hallucinated "shots=10" comes from treating busy zone
time as if every puck movement were a shot attempt. When you cannot
list a timestamp + position + release type for each claimed shot,
you are narrating, not counting. Drop to what you can enumerate.

### Consistency Rules

- shotsOnNet = saves + goals    (this is an identity, not a guideline)
  ⚠️  **DIRECTION MATTERS:** This identity is satisfied by adjusting `saves`,
  NEVER by inflating `goals`. If you have a SOG that you cannot link to a
  confirmed goal via a truth-table path, treat it as a save (saves += 1),
  not as a goal (goals += 1). See the ANTI-RATIONALIZATION RULE in the goal
  detection section above.
- shots >= shotsOnNet            (every shot on net is also a shot)
- rebounds <= saves              (rebounds come off saves)
- If no clear shot attempt is visible, all counts must be 0
- If `shots` exceeds 4 for a 120-second clip, you must be able to point
  to ≥4 distinct shooting motions that pass the "wind up, snap, or
  release" test from the shot definition. Otherwise reduce the count.
- If `shotsOnNet` is more than 70% of `shots`, you are likely
  over-counting SOG. Re-check whether each was actually a save.

### Final calibration check (before returning)

After computing the counts, ask yourself ALL of these:
1. Could a HUDL game tagger watching this clip live, pausing only at
   stoppages, plausibly have tagged this many shots?
2. For each shot you counted: can you list (a) a MM:SS timestamp in
   this clip, (b) the position on the ice the shot came from, and
   (c) the release type? If any shot fails this enumeration, drop it.
3. For each `shotOnNet` you counted: can you describe what the goalie
   did to handle it (glove, blocker, pad, stick, body, freeze)?
4. Does any sequence of "shots" in your count actually describe ONE
   originating shot followed by goalie scramble actions? If so, those
   scramble actions are not separate shots — collapse them into the
   originating shot.
5. Is your `shotsOnNet` count exactly equal to your `shots` count? If
   yes, you have probably treated misses-wide as SOG. Re-check whether
   any of your shots actually went wide of the net.
6. Does your decision_notes use phrases like "multiple shots on net" or
   "several saves" or "sustained pressure with shots" or "a flurry of
   shots"? Those are narrative summaries, not counts. Force yourself
   to enumerate the exact saves AND the exact shots; if you cannot
   enumerate them, lower your count.
7. Do four or more of your claimed shots cluster within a single
   30-second stretch of the clip? If yes, audit them: is this really
   four distinct wind-up/snap/release events from distinguishable
   shooters or positions, or are some of them scramble noise from a
   single originating shot? If you cannot defend each one as a
   distinct shooting motion, collapse the cluster.

If the answer to ANY check fails for one of your counts, drop that count.
**It is much better to UNDER-count than to inflate.**
